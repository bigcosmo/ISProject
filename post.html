The Loud, The Proud, The Philly Faithful

We have some of the loudest fans in all of professional sports. Just how loud do we get?

Posted by Luke Evans on April 17, 2024

Bedlam at the Bank. This simple phrase has come to describe one of the most electric postseason atmospheres in the MLB. Our Phillie fans are love their hometown team and they have the noise and the cheers to prove it.

The Experiment

During the 2023 postseason, the MLB wanted to run an experiment to see just how loud our Phillies fans got. During game 1 of the National League Championship Series (NLCS) against the Arizona Diamondbacks, MLB officials brought a decibel meter to Citizens Bank Park to measure the crowd noise during the game.

Philadelphia Phillies fans have been known to be some of the rowdiest fans in baseball

As is to be expected, crowd noise spiked when home runs were hit and during the final out. Here are the results:

  • Nick Castellanos’ home run: 103 decibels
  • Bryce Harper’s home run: 111 decibels
  • Kyle Schwarber’s home run: 108 decibels
  • Final out: 106 decibels

To give some context for just how loud these crowd reactions are, the whirring of a helicopter’s rotor blades are measured at 100 decibels, while a police siren or jet engine are measured at 120 decibels. That roar of the crowd in response to Bryce Harper’s home run came close to replicating the sound of a jet engine.

Next time you come to the park remember to bring some ear plugs… and your yell!
